# Interview Prep — Pearl Health

STAR story outlines, panel focus areas, and executive values alignment. Fill in your own examples.

---

## STAR format reminder

- **S**ituation — Context and stakes.
- **T**ask — Your responsibility.
- **A**ction — What you did (steps, decisions, collaboration).
- **R**esult — Outcome, metrics, or learning.

Keep each story to 2–3 minutes; practice so you can trim or expand.

---

## 4–5 stories to prepare

### 1. Applied AI project (design, challenges, outcome)

- **S**: [Product/team need: e.g. “We needed to let users ask questions over a large doc set.”]
- **T**: [Your role: e.g. “I owned the design and implementation of the RAG pipeline and integration.”]
- **A**: [Chunking/embedding/retrieval choices, observability, how you handled [latency/cost/quality].]
- **R**: [Outcome: e.g. “Shipped to production; X% adoption, latency under Y; we added evaluation and cost monitoring.”]

### 2. Cross-team or mentoring situation

- **S**: [e.g. “Team was new to LLMs; we had to ship a feature and raise quality bar.”]
- **T**: [Your role: e.g. “I led design and mentored two engineers on RAG and prompt engineering.”]
- **A**: [How you taught, reviewed designs/code, unblocked people, documented decisions.]
- **R**: [Team shipped on time; engineers could own similar work afterward.]

### 3. Reliability, incident, or performance fix

- **S**: [e.g. “Production LLM feature was slow / failing under load.”]
- **T**: [Your role: e.g. “I owned diagnosis and fix.”]
- **A**: [How you traced the issue, identified bottleneck (e.g. retrieval, API, timeouts), implemented fix and monitoring.]
- **R**: [Latency/error rate improved; we added alerts and runbooks.]

### 4. Ownership — discovery to delivery

- **S**: [Ambiguous or new product ask.]
- **T**: [You owned scope, design, and delivery.]
- **A**: [How you aligned with product, made tradeoffs, ran design review, shipped in iterations.]
- **R**: [Shipped on time; met success criteria; stakeholders aligned.]

### 5. Cross-functional (product, clinical, or compliance)

- **S**: [e.g. “We had to ship a feature that touched clinical workflow / compliance.”]
- **T**: [Your role in the cross-functional effort.]
- **A**: [How you worked with non-engineering: requirements, constraints, feedback loops, compromise.]
- **R**: [Shipped with requirements met; relationship with partner team improved.]

## Panel — what to expect

### Technical

- **System design**: e.g. “Design a service that uses LLMs to summarize patient data.” Emphasize: scalability, security (HIPAA, PHI), observability, and clear data flow. Use [technical-prep.md](technical-prep.md).
- **Live coding**: Clear variable names, edge cases, and a short complexity note. Discuss tradeoffs if asked.

### Behavioral

- **Themes**: Collaboration, transparency, “serious impact, big heart,” learning from others, raising quality.
- **Format**: Use STAR. Have 1–2 backup examples for “disagreement,” “failure,” “prioritization.”

### Cross-functional

- **Example**: Working with product, clinical, or compliance to ship a feature or meet a constraint. Focus on communication, constraints, and how you found a path to “yes.”

## Executive interview — values alignment

Prepare one short example per value (can be 1–2 minutes each).

### Collaborate to Innovate

- **Idea**: Best solutions come from teamwork; trust teammates; learn from each other; diverse perspectives.
- **Your example**: [Time you solved a hard problem by collaborating, incorporating feedback, or learning from a teammate’s approach.]

### Trust Through Transparency

- **Idea**: Transparency in interactions; integrity in how we build and communicate; open communication builds trust.
- **Your example**: [Time you were transparent about a risk, delay, or tradeoff with stakeholders or leadership and how it built trust.]

### Serious Impact, Big Heart

- **Idea**: Go above and beyond for proactive, patient-centered care; celebrate progress; humor and positivity.
- **Your example**: [Time you went the extra mile for users/patients or the team, or how you balance high impact with supporting people.]

## “Why Pearl” and “Why healthcare”

- **Why Pearl**: Value-based care, empowering primary care and physician-led networks; technology + financial tools + services; backed by strong investors; mission to improve outcomes and sustainability of American healthcare.
- **Why healthcare**: You want your engineering to improve patient outcomes and provider experience; you’re motivated by impact in a regulated, high-stakes domain; you’re interested in how AI can support (not replace) clinical judgment and proactive care.

## Questions to ask (3–5 per stage)

- How does the team balance speed of iteration with reliability and compliance for AI features?
- How do you measure success for an AI-powered feature (quality, adoption, clinical impact)?
- What does the roadmap look like for the AI intelligence layer over the next year?
- How does engineering work with product and clinical or compliance when defining AI features?
- What does “technical excellence” look like on this team, and how do you support growth in Applied AI?
